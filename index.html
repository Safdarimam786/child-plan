What is a Child Insurance Plan?

A child plan combines insurance and investment to help with a long-term financial planning for a child's future needs. The main goal
of such plan is to include an insurance component that will cover your child's requirements in the case of your death. These plans
have variable payments and can be used to pay for a child's education.

From premium payments to applicable charges, know all about insurance policies for your child before investing, you must understand the best benefits and features of a child plan. Let us begin.

Features of Child Plans

●     Financial Protection

Annuity child plans provide regular payments for the rest of your life after making a one-time deposit. Such life insurance annuities
provide a steady stream of funds for your child’s growth and education with no contribution limits.

●    Waiver of Premium Benefit

Premium waivers benefits get applied if the insured dies, or becomes unemployed, or is disabled within the specified term of
the policy and cannot pay the premiums. This feature of the child plans allows the child to receive the policy benefits even after the
premiums are waived off.

●     Sum Assured

Child plans offer a lump-sum payout of an amount guaranteed, equal to the maturity benefits due after the period. A maturity date
may be established according to your child's future financial needs, and the investment will continue to develop into a corpus during
the insurance period.

Benefits of Child Plans

●     Flexible Premium Payments

There is flexibility in paying a premium for child plans, which means the premium may be paid in three different ways. It can be made
in the form of a monthly pay premium payment, a single pay premium payment, or a limited pay premium payment. The individual's
chosen policy determines this. The premium amount is determined by the sum guaranteed and the maturity benefits of the selected
plan.

●    Death Benefits

One of the most important aspects of a child’s plan is that when a parent or legal guardian dies, the child receives a lump sum
payment against the insurance policy, but the insurer allows the continuation of investment plan for future maturity benefits.
This is because the insurance company understands that the parent’s death should not hamper the child's investing plan.

●     Nonforfeiture Benefits

These provisions in the child plan allow you to continue receiving benefits even if the policy has lapsed due to the nonpayment.
The sum might be a percentage of the premium, decreased benefits, or the policy's whole cash surrender value. Surrender and
premium discontinuation are examples of such advantages.

Tips to Pick the Best Child Plan

Choosing the correct strategy for your children's long-term development and growth is critical. The following are some of the child
insurance plan advices that you must follow to ensure that you select the ideal plan for your child-

●     Recognise the Requirements

You must understand the purpose of purchasing the plan-Is it for a specific reason or for a broad coverage. Knowing the correct goal
makes it simple to choose the best strategy for your child. Plans may be tailored to deliver the highest possible rewards based on the
child's future prospects.

●    Evaluation of the Risk Profile

The key to determine your risk-bearing capacity is evaluating and analysing your profile. Children Unit Linked Insurance Plans is a
good option if you want to take less risk. These plans provide returns that are tied to the stock market. You can also do transition
from an equity to a debt-based portfolio. Traditional Plans, which provide set returns, is a good choice if you want to make a secure
investment.

●     Weighing Benefits and Chargers

Not only you should look into the virtues of a child’s plan, but also analyse the costs associated with it. If a ULIP child insurance plan
is a possibility for you, you should read the small print and go through the prices under several headings. Make an investment decision
after assessing the advantages and disadvantages thoroughly.
